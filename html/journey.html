The Journey

The paths traveled to make us who we are.

Often the most key components to anyone's story are far more subtle than what their work defines, and for me defining myself by my work felt for a long time: false. My recent position at the Japanese Consulate has been the first position I've held that is truly matched to who I am, but before that much of my work was what I did rather than who I was. Even now my work still barely illuminates the values I actually hold most important.

And for me the first step is always guided by my family. I am a husband and a father to two daughter who complete my life. Life with a family today is chaotic, serene, expensive, time-consuming, energetic, exhausting, all-consuming, and giving. The reality is my wife and kids will always hold more of my thoughts, worries, and hopes than any other facet of my life. It wasn't always that way, but most of what I am is for them, even if I can't always give all of myself.

Hobbies are a funny thing. Early in my life they defined me, who I befriended, where I aimed. Now some of them are a complicated harness that supports while tieing me down. I love video games, I love travel, I love learning about other cultures and religions, I love reading a good fantasy or Sci-fi novel, I love snowboarding, backpacking, keeping on the cutting edge of technology, being uncomfortable in some foreign place but thriving in the stressfull moments of vacation before finding the peace that comes with relaxation.

This page is without question a ramble, but it exists to remind people, that PEOPLE, are much more than just the sum of their work experience, but are a creation much more complex. No journey alive is complete, and all of us are just walking some-direction one step at a time.
